About

I am a researcher interested in OS security, fuzzing, secure enclaves, virtualization, and reverse engineering. I have worked on mitigating memory-safety attacks, enabling reverse debugging for production failures, and fuzzing hypervisors for security-critical bugs. Besides research, I enjoy playing PC games (rampage knights, tomb raider, hades, ...), and I like the gaming industry so much that I developed a practical SGX-based anti-piracy solution that runs unmodified Windows games smoothly while hiding their plaintext code binary.

Before joining Microsoft Research, I received my Ph.D. in Computer Science and Engineering from Penn State in 2016, and my B.Eng. in Software Engineering from Nanjing University in 2012.
